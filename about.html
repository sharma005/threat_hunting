About ThreatFeed

A curated aggregator for actively-exploited vulnerabilities, zero-days, supply chain compromises, and ongoing attack campaigns — built and reviewed by Abhishek Sharma.

What this is

ThreatFeed pulls advisory- and CVE-focused threat intelligence from authoritative sources, normalizes each item into a common schema (title, category, severity, CVE, CVSS, actor, source, date), and publishes only what has been reviewed and approved. It is a fast, low-noise defensive reference — not a raw firehose.

Coverage

Actively Exploited (KEV)

CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog additions.

Zero-Day

Flaws exploited in the wild before or around patch availability.

Supply Chain

npm / PyPI / registry compromises, malicious packages, CI/CD attacks.

Ransomware / Campaign

Active ransomware groups and ongoing intrusion campaigns.

Critical RCE

High-impact remote code execution advisories (CVSS 9.x), patched but urgent.

Refresh & workflow

The feed re-pulls sources every 6 hours (4× daily). New candidate items are staged and surfaced for review — nothing is posted without explicit approval. Subscribe via the RSS/Atom feed at feed.xml.

Sources

  • CISA Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) & advisory alerts
  • Zero Day Initiative (ZDI) — published advisories & monthly review
  • Vendor PSIRTs & alerts — Oracle, Cisco, Microsoft, Google Chrome, Arista
  • Threat-intel reporting — Rapid7, SecurityWeek, The Hacker News, GitGuardian, Phoenix Security, CYFIRMA, Tenable, BleepingComputer

Notice

Items are aggregated from public advisories and reporting. IOCs and technical details are provided for defensive use only. Always verify against the linked primary source before acting.
